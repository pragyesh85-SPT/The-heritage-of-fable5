# W60 — Crypto Research (e.g. "Will Bitcoin go up or down?")

Trigger: any crypto price/outlook question.

## Rule zero
**Never output a prediction. Output a scenario framework** — current regime, bull case,
bear case, the levels that invalidate each, and what to WATCH. Anyone giving a single
confident direction is selling something. Also: every number must come from a live
fetch (prices/data change hourly); answering from memory is forbidden.

## The parameter stack (check in this order)

**1. Macro regime (sets the tide)**
- Fed/global rates direction + liquidity conditions; DXY (dollar strength inverse to BTC);
  risk appetite (equity indices trend). Crypto is a liquidity asset first.

**2. Structural/flow (the strongest medium-term signal now)**
- Spot ETF net flows (sustained inflow/outflow), stablecoin supply growth (dry powder),
  exchange BTC balances (falling = accumulation/self-custody).

**3. Cycle position**
- Where relative to the last halving; distance from all-time high; MVRV or
  realized-price ratio if available (over-/under-valued vs on-chain cost basis);
  long-term-holder behavior (distributing = late cycle).

**4. Derivatives (the short-term risk gauge)**
- Funding rates (heavily positive = crowded longs = flush risk), open interest vs price
  (OI spike without price progress = fragile), recent liquidation clusters (magnet levels).

**5. Technicals (levels, not fortune-telling — keep to 4)**
- Weekly trend vs 200-day MA; the 2–3 obvious support/resistance levels; RSI weekly for
  exhaustion; volume confirming or diverging from the move.

**6. Event calendar**
- Fed meetings, major regulatory decisions/court dates, large unlocks/legacy-estate
  distributions, options expiry clusters.

## Output format (always this shape)
1. Current price + regime in two lines (with data timestamps).
2. Bull case: drivers + the level/event that confirms it.
3. Bear case: drivers + the level that confirms it.
4. Invalidation: "the bull view is wrong below X because…".
5. Watchlist: the 3 data points to re-check and when.
6. The standing caveat, once, plainly: this is scenario analysis, not financial advice;
   position sizing (never more than one can lose on a volatile asset) matters more than
   direction calls.

## For altcoins add
Liquidity/volume reality (can you exit?), token unlock schedule (supply overhang),
whether it moves as anything other than a high-beta BTC proxy, team/treasury behavior.

## Failure this prevents
Confident single-direction calls from stale memory — the fastest way research loses
someone real money.
