# W62 — Researching Any Topic (General Method)

Trigger: "research X / what's the truth about Y / should I use Z" — anything outside
the specialized W60/W61 domains.

## Procedure
1. **Define the decision first.** Research serves a decision ("should I adopt X?",
   "how big is this market?"). Write the decision + what answer would change it. No
   decision = no research, just curiosity — say so and give a short answer.
2. **Decompose into 3–6 sub-questions** whose answers assemble the decision.
3. **Source rules:**
   - Prefer primary sources: official docs, filings, the actual paper, the actual
     pricing page — over blog summaries of them.
   - Every load-bearing claim needs 2 independent sources OR one primary source.
   - Note the DATE of every source; a 2023 answer to a 2026 question is a guess.
   - Distinguish clearly in the output: FACT (sourced) / ESTIMATE (derived, show the
     math) / OPINION (whose?).
4. **Steelman the other side.** Before concluding, search specifically for the best
   argument AGAINST the emerging conclusion ("X problems", "X vs alternative",
   "why not X"). Research that only searched one direction is confirmation shopping.
5. **Stop condition:** stop when new sources repeat what you have (saturation) or the
   remaining uncertainty no longer changes the decision. State what remains unknown.

## Output format
1. Answer to the decision in 2–3 sentences.
2. The key findings, each with its source and date.
3. The strongest counter-argument and why it does/doesn't change the answer.
4. Confidence: high/medium/low + what single new fact would flip the conclusion.

## Failure this prevents
Confident summaries of stale blog posts, one-sided research, facts/estimates/opinions
blended into mush, research with no decision attached.
